IMPLIMENTATION OF REGULATIONS FOR KAROO CENTRAL ASTRONOMY ADVANTAGE AREAS

Core Astronomy Advantage Area is the physical area in which most of observatory or instruments are located

Central Astronomy Advantage Areas is the area where the Minister restrict radio spectrum use that has detrimental use on astronomy

Coordinated Astronomy Advantage Area is the area where the Minister sets standards and conditions to prevent detrimental impact on astronomy

Buffers includes 20 KM radius for saturation and 50 KM radius that ICASA exempted spectrum require a permit

Corridors include 10 KM for saturation, 30 KM for electrical equipment, 50 KM for wind turbines

The AGA legislation in South Africa preserve and protect areas that are uniquely suited for radio astronomy

African patner countries are Botswana, Ghana, Kenya, Madagascar, Mauritius, Mozambique, Namibia, South Africa and Zambia

The whole territory excluding Sol Plaatje is protected for radio astronomy purpose

The radio frequency spectrum from 100 MHz to 25.5 GHz is restricted for astronomy purpose

The circle with a 50 KM radius identifies the area that ICASA exempted spectrum require a permit

The location for the remote telescope stations in the SKA spiral arm

Core AAA is a physical area in which most of the telescope stations are located.

Central AAAs is where the Minister restrict the use of radio frequency spectrum that has detrimental impact on astronomy.

Coordinated AAA is where the Minister sets standards and conditions to prevent detrimental impact on astronomy.

20 KM buffer is the circle with a 20 KM radius to determine the saturation level.

10 KM protection corridor is the spiral arms area to determine the saturation level for radio transmitters and SARAS level for electrical equipment.

Permit is required for radio equipment using ICASA exempted spectrum within a 50 KM radius.

Permit is required for electrical equipment above 100 kVA within a distance of 30 km from the nearest SKA Infrastructure Territory corridor.

Permit is required for wind turbine electricity generation facility within a distance of 50 km from the nearest SKA Infrastructure Territory corridor.

Kern AAA is 'n fisiese gebied waarin die meeste van die teleskoop stasies geleë is.

Sentraal-AAAs is waar die Minister die gebruik van radiofrekwensiespektrum beperk wat nadelige impak op sterrekunde het.

Gekoördineerde AAA is waar die Minister standaarde en voorwaardes stel om nadelige impak op sterrekunde te voorkom.

20 KM buffer is die sirkel met 'n 20 KM radius om die versadigingsvlak te bepaal.

10 KM beskerming korridor is die spiraal wapen gebied om die versadiging vlak vir radio sesenders en SARAS vlak vir elektriese toerusting te bepaal.

Permit word benodig vir radiotoerusting deur ICASA vrygestelde spektrum binne 'n 50 KM-radius te gebruik.

Permit word benodig vir elektriese toerusting bo 100 kVA binne 'n afstand van 30 km vanaf die naaste SKA Infrastruktuur gebied korridor.

Permit word benodig vir windturbine elektrisiteitsopwekkingsfasiliteit binne 'n afstand van 50 km vanaf die naaste SKA Infrastruktuurgebied korridor.

The radio telescope should be located in the area that has little or no interference.

Cosmic radio signal detected and measured by radio telescopes are weaker than manmade radio transmission or electrical emission.

A cellular telephone located on the Moon would produce a signal on Earth that radio astronomers consider quite strong.

The radio telescope interference can be caused by terrestrial transmissions reflected by the Moon.

Radio astronomy interference leads to both data loss and loss of data quality.

The risk classification of radio transmission and electrical emission varies between extreme risk, high risk, medium risk and low risk.

Radio transmission and electrical emission in the core area and some of the servitude farms poses an extreme risk of interference.

Radio transmission and electrical emission within the 20 KM radius and some of servitude farms poses high risk of interference.

Radio transmission and electrical emission within the 50 KM radius, KCAAA3 and KCAAA2 poses medium risk of interference.

Radio transmission and electrical emission within in the KCAAA1 pose a low risk of interreference.

Die radio teleskoop moet geleë wees in die gebied wat min of geen inmenging.

Kosmiese radio sein opgespoor en gemeet deur radio teleskope is swakker as mensgemaakte radio-oordrag of elektriese emissie.

'N Sellulêre telefoon geleë op die maan sou 'n sein op aarde wat radio sterrekundiges oorweeg baie sterk.

Die radio teleskoop inmenging kan veroorsaak word deur aarstriële uitsendings weerspieël deur die Maan.

Radiosterrekunde-inmenging lei tot beide dataverlies en verlies van datakwaliteit.

Die risikoklasssifikasie van radio-oordrag en elektriese uitstoot wissel tussen uiterste risiko, hoë risiko, medium risiko en lae risiko.

Radio-oordrag en elektriese uitstoot in die kerngebied en sommige van die serwituutplase hou 'n uiters risiko van inmenging in.

Radio-oordrag en elektriese uitstoot binne die 20 KM-radius en sommige van serwituutplase hou hoë risiko van inmenging in.

Radio-oordrag en elektriese uitstoot binne die 50 KM-radius, KCAAA3 en KCAAA2 hou mediumrisiko van inmenging in.

Radio-oordrag en elektriese uitstoot binne die KCAAA1 hou 'n lae risiko van inmenging in.
